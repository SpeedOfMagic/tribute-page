Mirzakhani Maryam

(5.3.1977-7.14.2017)

'A hardworking genius.'

- Mehrdokht Pournader, the PhD candidate.

Maryam Mirza-Khani was an Iranian-American mathematician and a professor of mathematics at Stanford University. On 13th of August, 2014 she became the first woman and the first Iranian, who got the most prestigious award in mathematics - Fields medal.

Unfortunately, her life didn't last long: she died of breast cancer at the age of 40. Nevertheless, she became an inspiration to young mathematicians after that.

If you want to know more about this person, you can visit various web-sites, such as Wikipedia or The Conversation.
